Puzzles, puzzles

I’m a human who craves puzzles to solve. It’s a trope at this point; the puzzle-hungry genius, but, my puzzles seem to be unlike anything anyone else has ever seen. The puzzles I crave are so much larger, and usually assembled upside-down, so to speak; I almost never know what I’m putting together until it’s all in place. I haven’t given it much thought, in the meta, before now. The lens of genius in the modern social eye focuses on obvious puzzles, such as crime and mathematics, but being intelligent has two very expensive necessities: the acceptance and the audience. The audience is important but usually easy to find; without anyone to show one’s genius, there’s nothing with which to compare it. The acceptance, though, that is perhaps where I differ from others of intelligence — I didn’t have a proud youth to teach me to accept the world around me as actively under my intelligent control. Instead, acceptance had to come to me in the form of coping.
