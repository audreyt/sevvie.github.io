A Smaller Update

I've temporarily switched over to Jekyll for developing my blog, though I'm not really using it the way Github wants me to, letting it be compiled on the server-side. Instead I use jekyll watch to auto-build into my sevvie.github.io directory, and write away. I also make use of a _drafts directory, to store partial thoughts in for later editing and work.

All that being said; I can't stand Jekyll. It feels so half-assed, compared to what it could be if given some love, some compersion from a passionate individual or group of individuals. Even Ruhoh, which wants to solve Jekyll's problems, feels dry and limited, to me. Static Blog Generators should be one of the next steps forward, given the nature of the web today; with the numerous services today providing the extensions of blogging, like Disqus, Twitter and Facebook, Search Engine Webmaster Tools with Google and Bing, and the availability of important APIs like Statistics Tracking, one barely needs a backend for a basic blog anymore. What they need, however, is "integration" of important modern aspects of the web, such as automatically embedding video links from YouTube and Vimeo or audio from Spotify, making use of JavaScript on the frontend (but only where necessary), and the extensibility to be able to add their own ideas, however one needs them.

Until then, however, I'm still a person with no code to show for my words. There is a little shame in that; however, it's a waste of my energy to feel that shame because, as weird as it might be, I have to talk through my thoughts -- if even just in a blog -- to get them shaped up enough to put into code. If you find that interesting, that's awesome. If not? I'll just get back to my code, then.
