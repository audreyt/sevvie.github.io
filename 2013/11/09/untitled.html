This began as a moment of free rambling at a beloved of mine, over texts. There aren't many people in the world with whom I find comfort in their terseness but, when he is of few words... I feel like I've said something correct. There is a strange and wonderful beauty, though, to our respective geniuses -- especially when they dance together like a double helix climbing toward greater understandings. I don't know about him, but that genius has always come with an introspective question, and that question is always the same: "Will I always be like this?" The answer, this time, is different.

"Yes, isn't it grand? Isn't it free?"

At eleven years old, I was being bred for an entrepreneurial future by both the best and the shadiest of businessmen and white collar criminals -- and I was soaking it up like a sponge. I was far too intelligent to be exposed to some of the things to which I was; it sublimated and drifted up into my nose and imprinted itself on my strongest memories. And both the upright and the criminal showed themselves quite well, as I became more than those businessmen could handle. By 17 years old, I had been in a military school for a number of years, now being reared with a focus on a different selection and cultural lens on my skills. Suffice it to say, this corrupt and abusive military school were the first in a number of people who wished to weaponize the brilliance and grace they had stumbled upon.

After it all, however, I made the youthful and exuberant choice of attempting to handle absolutely all of it on my own. I tried to make myself human, and I availed myself of all of the skills I had learned prior to accomplish that. Watching everyone around me and learning from every faith and religion I could study led to stunning misunderstandings and even forgeries of self -- taking me to a place where getting into fights and chasing adrenaline highs caused numerous injuries and memory problems, many of which still unsolved. And here I stand now, after months, even years, of breaking myself down, observing myself, fixing habits and accepting who I am, what I am, where I am.

Mind, Body, Soul

Despite the mistakes and outright horrid choices I've made in my past, I see a success amongst the chaos -- I did, in fact, succeed in making a whole person out of myself. It was an ugly success, but a success nonetheless.

The past, though, is just wood, steel, iron, and other structural carbons; it is what builds the temple that is oneself. We all have flaws in our pasts, failings and faults. A roof and attic requires supporting walls, and if you try to fix one wall, you'll likely need to add additional supports to maintain the structure during repairs. However, there is more to a home than just the walls and roof. A proper home has a yard, a garden -- a place that connects the structure with the rest of the world around it, while setting clear boundaries. It is from where your guests approach; it is natural and not completely under one's control, with beautiful trees making their own patterns and marks apparent.

Perhaps the body and soul's front yard is merely the interactions with which we fill our days; the sharp and chaotic or calm and well-treated bushes and trees growing right out front and yet, we notice them less and less as we adapt to living with them. But even the smallest change is important -- trimming the rose bush keeps it growing dense and beautiful, and cutting the limbs from some trees keeps them from reaching into other people's yards and creating conflict.

We'll See, said the Blind Zen Master

It is with patience, and observation, that I take a remarkably horrible situation and begin to shape lemonade off this tree of lemons I've crashed into. A rather spacious 2-bedroom unit in a duplex, and the lower-level unit, that comes with the baggage of previous tenant's utility bills which went unpaid for nearly a year, with a gorgeous yard in which Elty adores playing; this is what will soon be my home.
